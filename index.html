Charles Bonnet Syndrome

The Hallucinations of the Sane

Discovered in 1760 by the Swiss naturalist Charles Bonnet, this syndrome affects those who have lost much or all of their vision. As AMD damages the macula, creating blind spots or severe vision loss, the brain attempts to fill in the missing visual information. This results in vivid, complex, and sometimes startling hallucinations.

In our VR simulation, you can experience firsthand how these phantom images appear dynamically over your real-world view or within a simulated environment.

Understanding the Degradation

Age-related Macular Degeneration physically alters how the eye captures light, leading to profound effects.

Central Blind Spot

A darkening or blurring at the very center of your visual field, expanding as the disease progresses.

Tunnel Vision

While often associated with Glaucoma, advanced vision loss can lead to severely restricted peripheral or central awareness.
